## Part 1: Data Interpretation (Questions 1 to 5)

**Context given:**
The following table shows the cumulative percentage of the total number of students appearing in the Hindi and Punjabi examination and the cumulative number of students appearing in the Hindi examination and scoring marks in five different categories, namely, 80 and above; 70 and above; 60 and above; 50 and above; and 40 and above.

| Marks (Out of 100) | Cumulative percentage of the total number of students who appeared in Hindi and Punjabi | Cumulative number of students who appeared in Hindi |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 80 and above | 15% | 108 |
| 70 and above | 35% | 264 |
| 60 and above | 65% | 424 |
| 50 and above | 90% | 554 |
| 40 and above | 100% | 624 |

*Note:*
*(1) Total number of students who attended the exam is 1200.*
*(2) No student failed in the examination.*
*(3) Each student appeared in only one exam, that is, either in Hindi or in Punjabi exam.*

> [!TIP] 
> **Shortcut for all DI questions:** First, decode the cumulative table into exact brackets. Since 100% is "40 and above" and no student failed, the total students in Hindi is 624. Thus, Punjabi students = 1200 - 624 = 576. Let's calculate the exact students in each bracket:
> 
> | Marks Range | Total Students (from Cumulative %) | Hindi Students | Punjabi Students (Total - Hindi) |
> | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
> | **80-100** | 15% of 1200 = **180** | **108** | 180 - 108 = **72** |
> | **70-79** | (35% - 15%) = 20% of 1200 = **240** | 264 - 108 = **156** | 240 - 156 = **84** |
> | **60-69** | (65% - 35%) = 30% of 1200 = **360** | 424 - 264 = **160** | 360 - 160 = **200** |
> | **50-59** | (90% - 65%) = 25% of 1200 = **300** | 554 - 424 = **130** | 300 - 130 = **170** |
> | **40-49** | (100% - 90%) = 10% of 1200 = **120** | 624 - 554 = **70** | 120 - 70 = **50** |


### **Question 1**
**Question:** What is the sum of the number of students who scored 70 or more than 70 but less than 80 marks in Hindi and the number of students who scored 80 or more than 80 marks in Punjabi?
**Options:**
(1) 330
(2) 228
(3) 272
(4) 284

**Step-by-step Solution:**
1. **Students scoring 70-79 in Hindi**: Find the difference between cumulative students scoring "70 and above" and "80 and above" in Hindi. $264 - 108 = 156$.
2. **Students scoring 80 and above in Punjabi**: Find total students scoring 80 and above: $15\%$ of $1200 = 180$. Subtract the Hindi students in this range: $180 - 108 = 72$.
3. **Sum** = $156 + 72 = 228$.

**Shortcut:** Refer to the pre-calculated table. Hindi (70-79) is **156**. Punjabi (80-100) is **72**. $156 + 72 = 228$. 
**Correct Option is (2)**.

### **Question 5**
**Question:** What is the average of the number of students who scored 50 or more than 50 marks but less than 60 marks in Punjabi and the number of students who scored 50 or more than 50 marks but less than 60 marks in Hindi?

## Part 2: Mathematical Statistics

### **Question 13**
**Question:** Standard deviation (S) and coefficient of Variation (CV Expressed as a percentage) of different datasets are given in A-D, below. Compute the mean of each dataset and arrange in ascending order.
A. $S = 4, CV = 40/9$
B. $S = 5, CV = 6.25$
C. $S = 14, CV = 70/3$
D. $S = 16, CV = 160/7$
Choose the correct answer from the options given below:
(1) C, B, D, A
(2) C, D, B, A
(3) A, D, B, C
(4) D, B, C, A

**Step-by-step Solution:**
1. The formula for Coefficient of Variation (CV) as a percentage is: $CV = (\frac{\text{Standard Deviation}}{\text{Mean}}) \times 100$.
2. Rearranging, we get $\text{Mean} = (\frac{\text{Standard Deviation}}{CV}) \times 100$.
3. **Calculate Mean for A:** $\text{Mean}_A = (\frac{4}{40/9}) \times 100 = \frac{4 \times 9}{40} \times 100 = \frac{36}{40} \times 100 = 90$.
4. **Calculate Mean for B:** $\text{Mean}_B = (\frac{5}{6.25}) \times 100 = (\frac{500}{625}) \times 100 = 0.8 \times 100 = 80$.
5. **Calculate Mean for C:** $\text{Mean}_C = (\frac{14}{70/3}) \times 100 = \frac{14 \times 3}{70} \times 100 = \frac{42}{70} \times 100 = 0.6 \times 100 = 60$.
6. **Calculate Mean for D:** $\text{Mean}_D = (\frac{16}{160/7}) \times 100 = \frac{16 \times 7}{160} \times 100 = \frac{112}{160} \times 100 = 0.7 \times 100 = 70$.
7. **Ascending order of means:** 60 (C), 70 (D), 80 (B), 90 (A). The order is C, D, B, A.

**Shortcut:** Since $\text{Mean} = 100 \times \frac{S}{CV}$, just quickly simplify the ratio $\frac{S}{CV}$:
- A: $4 \div (40/9) = 36/40 = 0.9$
- B: $5 \div 6.25 = 5 \div (25/4) = 20/25 = 0.8$
- C: $14 \div (70/3) = 42/70 = 0.6$
- D: $16 \div (160/7) = 112/160 = 0.7$
Order is C (0.6) < D (0.7) < B (0.8) < A (0.9). 
**Correct Option is (2)**.

### **Question 15**
**Question:** Match List I with List II:
| List I [Population mean ($\mu$) and $\frac{1}{N}\sum x_i^2$] | List II [Population standard deviation ($\sigma$)] |
| :--- | :--- |
| A. $\mu = 9, \frac{1}{N}\sum x_i^2 = 117$ | I. $10$ |
| B. $\mu = 10, \frac{1}{N}\sum x_i^2 = 200$ | II. $4$ |
| C. $\mu = 11, \frac{1}{N}\sum x_i^2 = 202$ | III. $6$ |
| D. $\mu = 12, \frac{1}{N}\sum x_i^2 = 160$ | IV. $9$ |

Choose the correct answer from the options given below:
(1) A-III, B-IV, C-I, D-II
(2) A-II, B-I, C-IV, D-III
(3) A-III, B-I, C-IV, D-II
(4) A-I, B-IV, C-II, D-III

**Step-by-step Solution:**
1. The formula for Population Variance is $\sigma^2 = \frac{1}{N}\sum x_i^2 - \mu^2$. Standard deviation is $\sigma = \sqrt{\sigma^2}$.
2. **For A:** $\sigma^2 = 117 - 9^2 = 117 - 81 = 36 \Rightarrow \sigma = 6$. So, A matches III.
3. **For B:** $\sigma^2 = 200 - 10^2 = 200 - 100 = 100 \Rightarrow \sigma = 10$. So, B matches I.
4. **For C:** $\sigma^2 = 202 - 11^2 = 202 - 121 = 81 \Rightarrow \sigma = 9$. So, C matches IV.
5. **For D:** $\sigma^2 = 160 - 12^2 = 160 - 144 = 16 \Rightarrow \sigma = 4$. So, D matches II.
6. The correct matching is A-III, B-I, C-IV, D-II.

**Shortcut:** Just calculate for A and B to eliminate options. A is $\sqrt{117 - 81} = \sqrt{36} = 6$ (III). This leaves options (1) and (3). B is $\sqrt{200 - 100} = \sqrt{100} = 10$ (I). Only Option (3) has A-III, B-I. 
**Correct Option is (3)**.

## Part 3: Mathematical Reasoning and Aptitude

### **Question 21**
**Question:** Find the number that can replace question mark (?) in the series given below.
7, 12, 32, 122, ?, 3602, 25202
**Options:**
(1) 602
(2) 610
(3) 702
(4) 622

**Step-by-step Solution:**
1. Analyze the progression between consecutive terms. The series grows very rapidly, suggesting multiplication.
2. Let's test multipliers:
    *   $7 \times 2 = 14$ (subtract $2$ to get $12$) $\Rightarrow 7 \times 2 - 2 = 12$
    *   $12 \times 3 = 36$ (subtract $4$ to get $32$) $\Rightarrow 12 \times 3 - 4 = 32$
    *   $32 \times 4 = 128$ (subtract $6$ to get $122$) $\Rightarrow 32 \times 4 - 6 = 122$
3. The pattern is clear: Multiply by an increasing integer ($2, 3, 4, 5 \dots$) and subtract an increasing even number ($2, 4, 6, 8 \dots$).
4. Applying the pattern to find the missing term: $122 \times 5 - 8 = 610 - 8 = 602$.
5. Let's verify with the next term: $602 \times 6 - 10 = 3612 - 10 = 3602$. The pattern holds.
6. The missing number is 602.

**Shortcut:** Identify the operation $n \times k - 2(k-1)$ early. For the transition from $122$, it is simply $122 \times 5 - 8$. Calculating $122 \times 5$ is easy: $1220 / 2 = 610$. Subtracting 8 gives $602$. 
**Correct Option is (1)**.

**Question:** In a certain code language:
'NOIDA' is written as : 1, 4, 9, 15, 14
'INDIA' is written as : 1, 9, 4, 14, 9
How 'HELLO' will be written in this language?
**Options:**

### **Question 23**
**Question:** Which of the following statement(s) is/are wrong?
A. A man runs at a speed of 20 km/hr and covers a distance of 400 m. The time taken by the man to cover 400 m is 8 minutes.
B. A person covers a certain distance at x km/hr and an equal distance at y km/hr. The average speed during the whole journey is $\frac{2xy}{x+y}$ km/hr.
C. A Cyclist covers a distance of 750 m in $2\frac{1}{2}$ minutes. The speed of the cyclist is 18 km/hr.
D. A speed of x km/hr is equal to $\frac{5x}{18}$ m/sec.

Choose the most appropriate answer from the options given below:
(1) A Only
(2) A and B Only
(3) A and C Only
(4) B, C and D Only

**Step-by-step Solution:**
1. **Check Statement A:**
    *   Speed = $20 \text{ km/hr} = 20 \times (\frac{5}{18}) \text{ m/sec} = \frac{100}{18} \text{ m/sec} = \frac{50}{9} \text{ m/sec}$.
    *   Distance = $400 \text{ m}$.
    *   Time = $\frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{400}{50/9} = \frac{400 \times 9}{50} = 8 \times 9 = 72 \text{ seconds}$.
    *   $72 \text{ seconds} = \frac{72}{60} \text{ minutes} = 1.2 \text{ minutes}$.
    *   Statement A says the time taken is 8 minutes, which is **Wrong**.
2. **Check Statement B:**
    *   This is the standard formula for average speed (harmonic mean) when equal distances are covered at two different speeds.
    *   Let distance be $D$. Total distance = $2D$. Time 1 = $D/x$, Time 2 = $D/y$.
    *   Average Speed = $\frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}} = \frac{2D}{\frac{D}{x} + \frac{D}{y}} = \frac{2}{\frac{x+y}{xy}} = \frac{2xy}{x+y}$.
    *   Statement B is **Correct**.
3. **Check Statement C:**
    *   Distance = $750 \text{ m} = 0.75 \text{ km}$.
    *   Time = $2.5 \text{ minutes} = \frac{2.5}{60} \text{ hours} = \frac{1}{24} \text{ hours}$.
    *   Speed = $\frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} = \frac{0.75}{1/24} = 0.75 \times 24 = \frac{3}{4} \times 24 = 18 \text{ km/hr}$.
    *   Statement C is **Correct**.
4. **Check Statement D:**
    *   To convert km/hr to m/sec, we multiply by $\frac{1000 \text{ m}}{3600 \text{ sec}} = \frac{5}{18}$.
    *   Therefore, $x \text{ km/hr} = x \times \frac{5}{18} \text{ m/sec} = \frac{5x}{18} \text{ m/sec}$.
    *   Statement D is **Correct**.
5. Only statement A is wrong.

**Shortcut:** Statement D is a basic conversion fact. Statement B is the standard harmonic mean formula. In A, $20 \text{ km/hr}$ is roughly $3 \text{ min per km}$. To run $400 \text{m}$ (less than half a km), it will take roughly $1.2 \text{ minutes}$. 8 minutes is completely wrong ($8 \text{ min} \times 20 \text{ km/hr} \approx 2.6 \text{ km}$). A is obviously false. 
**Correct Option is (1)**.

### **Question 24**
**Question:** When a producer allows 36% commission on the retail price of his product, he earns a profit of 8.8%. What would be his profit if the commission is reduced by 24%?
**Options:**
(1) 36.6%
(2) 49.6%
(3) 59.6%
(4) 45.6%

**Step-by-step Solution:**
1. Let the Retail Price (Marked Price) be $R = 100$.
2. Commission allowed = $36\% \text{ of } 100 = 36$.
3. Selling Price ($SP_1$) = $R - \text{Commission} = 100 - 36 = 64$.
4. Profit is given as $8.8\%$. This means $SP_1$ is $108.8\%$ of Cost Price (CP).
    *   $SP_1 = CP \times 1.088$
    *   $64 = CP \times 1.088$
    *   $CP = \frac{64}{1.088} = \frac{64000}{1088} = \frac{1000}{17}$ (dividing numerator and denominator by 64).
5. Now, the commission is reduced *by* 24%. This implies the new commission is $36\% - 24\% = 12\%$.
    *   New Commission = $12\% \text{ of } 100 = 12$.
    *   New Selling Price ($SP_2$) = $100 - 12 = 88$.
6. New Profit = $SP_2 - CP = 88 - \frac{1000}{17} = \frac{1496 - 1000}{17} = \frac{496}{17}$.
7. New Profit Percentage = $(\frac{\text{New Profit}}{CP}) \times 100\% = \frac{496/17}{1000/17} \times 100\% = (\frac{496}{1000}) \times 100\% = 49.6\%$.

**Shortcut:** We can use the equivalence ratio: $\frac{100 + P_1}{100 - D_1} = \frac{100 + P_2}{100 - D_2}$.
$\frac{108.8}{100 - 36} = \frac{100 + P_2}{100 - 12}$
$\frac{108.8}{64} = \frac{100 + P_2}{88}$
$100 + P_2 = \frac{108.8}{64} \times 88 = \frac{108.8}{8} \times 11 = 13.6 \times 11 = 149.6$
$P_2 = 149.6 - 100 = 49.6\%$. 
**Correct Option is (2)**.

### **Question 25**
**Question:** A sum was put at simple interest at a certain rate for 3 years, Had it been put at 1% higher rate, it would have fetched ₹5100 more. Find the sum.
**Options:**
(1) ₹ 170,000
(2) ₹ 150,000
(3) ₹ 175,000
(4) ₹ 200,000

**Step-by-step Solution:**
1. Let the sum (Principal) be $P$. Let the original interest rate be $R\%$ per annum. Time ($T$) = 3 years.
2. Original Simple Interest ($SI_1$) = $\frac{P \times R \times 3}{100}$.
3. New rate = $(R + 1)\%$.
4. New Simple Interest ($SI_2$) = $\frac{P \times (R + 1) \times 3}{100}$.
5. Given difference: $SI_2 - SI_1 = 5100$.
    *   $\frac{P \times (R + 1) \times 3}{100} - \frac{P \times R \times 3}{100} = 5100$
    *   $\frac{3P(R + 1 - R)}{100} = 5100$
    *   $\frac{3P}{100} = 5100$
6. Solving for P:
    *   $3P = 5100 \times 100$
    *   $P = \frac{510000}{3} = 170,000$.

**Shortcut:** The extra 1% interest rate for 3 years simply adds a total of $1\% \times 3 = 3\%$ of the Principal as extra interest. This 3% of Principal corresponds to the extra amount fetched, which is ₹5100.
$3\% \text{ of Sum} = 5100$.
$1\% \text{ of Sum} = 1700$.
$100\% \text{ of Sum} = 170,000$. 
**Correct Option is (1)**.

### **Question 21**
**Question:** A milkman buys 20 litres of milk from a dairy at the cost of Rs. 50 per litre. He also purchases some mineral water at the rate of Rs 20 per litre and adds it to the milk.
